# FERPA Compliance Checklist

A practical, actionable checklist for educational institutions, EdTech vendors, and service providers preparing for or maintaining compliance with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA).

Last Reviewed: March 2026

---

## Governance and Policy

- [ ] Designate a FERPA compliance officer (or assign FERPA responsibilities to a specific role)
- [ ] Develop a comprehensive FERPA policy covering all areas of student data privacy
- [ ] Publish the FERPA policy and make it accessible to students, parents, and staff
- [ ] Review and update the FERPA policy at least annually
- [ ] Establish a FERPA governance committee with representatives from IT, legal, registrar, and academic affairs
- [ ] Document the institution's definition of "education records"
- [ ] Document the institution's criteria for "school officials with legitimate educational interest"
- [ ] Develop procedures for handling FERPA complaints internally
- [ ] Maintain records of all FERPA-related policy decisions and approvals

---

## Annual Notification

- [ ] Issue annual FERPA notification to all parents and eligible students
- [ ] Include in the notification: right to inspect records, right to request amendment, right to consent to disclosure, right to file a complaint
- [ ] Include the institution's directory information designations in the notification
- [ ] Provide a clear and accessible opt-out mechanism for directory information
- [ ] Specify the deadline for opting out of directory information disclosure
- [ ] Deliver the notification through an appropriate method (mail, email, student portal, handbook)
- [ ] Document that notification was issued and maintain records of delivery
- [ ] Update the notification annually to reflect any changes in policy or practice

---

## Records Access and Amendment

- [ ] Establish a process for parents/eligible students to request access to education records
- [ ] Respond to access requests within 45 days
- [ ] Allow inspection and review of records at a convenient time and place
- [ ] Do not charge fees for searching or retrieving records (copying fees are permissible)
- [ ] Establish a process for parents/eligible students to request amendment of records
- [ ] Respond to amendment requests within a reasonable time
- [ ] Provide a formal hearing process if an amendment request is denied
- [ ] Allow placement of a statement in the record if the hearing upholds the institution's decision
- [ ] Train registrar and records staff on access and amendment procedures
- [ ] Maintain a log of all access requests and their outcomes

---

## Consent and Disclosure

- [ ] Obtain written consent before disclosing PII from education records (unless an exception applies)
- [ ] Ensure consent forms include: the specific records to be disclosed, the purpose, and the recipient
- [ ] Document all disclosures of education records (except disclosures to the student or disclosures of directory information)
- [ ] Maintain a disclosure log for each student's record
- [ ] Retain disclosure records for as long as the education records themselves are maintained
- [ ] Verify the identity of all parties requesting education records
- [ ] Document which FERPA exception applies for each non-consensual disclosure
- [ ] Establish procedures for responding to subpoenas and court orders (including notification to students)
- [ ] Train staff on when consent is required and when exceptions apply

---

## Directory Information

- [ ] Define what the institution considers directory information
- [ ] Limit directory information designations to the minimum necessary
- [ ] Maintain a centralized opt-out list
- [ ] Ensure the opt-out list is consulted before any directory information disclosure
- [ ] Integrate the opt-out list with all systems that may disclose directory information
- [ ] Train staff on directory information policies and opt-out procedures
- [ ] Review directory information designations annually
- [ ] Consider whether to implement a "limited directory information" approach for specific purposes

---

## Technology and EdTech Vendors

- [ ] Inventory all technology platforms and vendors with access to education records
- [ ] Classify each vendor by the type and volume of education records accessed
- [ ] Execute a compliant agreement with each vendor before sharing education records
- [ ] Ensure vendor agreements designate the vendor as a "school official"
- [ ] Ensure vendor agreements specify the legitimate educational interest served
- [ ] Ensure vendor agreements prohibit use of education records beyond the specified purpose
- [ ] Ensure vendor agreements prohibit re-disclosure of education records without authorization
- [ ] Ensure vendor agreements address data security, breach notification, and data retention/destruction
- [ ] Review vendor security certifications (SOC 2, HITRUST, ISO 27001)
- [ ] Conduct periodic reviews of vendor compliance with agreement terms
- [ ] Establish a process for revoking vendor access when services are terminated
- [ ] Require vendors to return or destroy education records upon contract termination
- [ ] Implement a technology vetting process that includes FERPA review before adoption
- [ ] Educate faculty and staff about the risks of adopting technology without IT/procurement review

---

## Access Controls

- [ ] Implement role-based access controls (RBAC) for all student information systems
- [ ] Enforce the principle of least privilege for all accounts accessing education records
- [ ] Require unique user credentials for all accounts (no shared or generic accounts)
- [ ] Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) for systems containing education records
- [ ] Disable access for terminated employees within 24 hours
- [ ] Conduct access reviews at least semi-annually for student information systems
- [ ] Document and log all access to education records
- [ ] Implement session timeout for inactive sessions in student information systems
- [ ] Restrict administrative access to student information systems to authorized IT staff
- [ ] Test access controls periodically to verify enforcement

---

## Data Security

- [ ] Encrypt education records in transit (TLS 1.2 or higher)
- [ ] Encrypt education records at rest (database encryption, full-disk encryption)
- [ ] Implement data loss prevention (DLP) controls to prevent unauthorized exfiltration
- [ ] Deploy endpoint protection on all devices with access to education records
- [ ] Implement network segmentation to isolate student information systems
- [ ] Conduct vulnerability scans on systems containing education records at least quarterly
- [ ] Conduct annual penetration testing that includes student information systems
- [ ] Implement secure backup and recovery procedures for education records
- [ ] Monitor for unauthorized access to education records
- [ ] Implement secure disposal procedures for devices and media containing education records

---

## Audit Logging and Monitoring

- [ ] Enable audit logging for all access to and disclosure of education records
- [ ] Capture the following in logs: user identity, timestamp, action, records accessed, success/failure
- [ ] Centralize log collection using a SIEM or log management platform
- [ ] Monitor logs for suspicious activity (bulk exports, after-hours access, unauthorized access attempts)
- [ ] Set up automated alerts for high-risk events
- [ ] Retain logs for a minimum of one year (longer if required by state law)
- [ ] Protect log integrity with write-once storage or equivalent controls
- [ ] Review audit logs regularly (at least monthly for student information systems)
- [ ] Include audit log review in the institution's regular security assessment process

---

## Training and Awareness

- [ ] Provide FERPA training to all employees with access to education records
- [ ] Deliver FERPA training at hire and annually thereafter
- [ ] Include FERPA-specific content in general security awareness training
- [ ] Provide role-specific training (registrar staff, IT, faculty, advisors, administrators)
- [ ] Cover the following topics: education records definition, disclosure rules, consent requirements, directory information, exceptions, incident reporting
- [ ] Train staff on the proper handling of student records in email, cloud storage, and paper files
- [ ] Train staff on how to identify and report potential FERPA violations
- [ ] Document all training activities and maintain attendance records
- [ ] Test training effectiveness through quizzes or simulated scenarios
- [ ] Update training content annually to address new risks and regulatory changes

---

## Incident Response

- [ ] Develop an incident response plan that specifically addresses breaches of education records
- [ ] Identify your state's student data breach notification requirements
- [ ] Establish notification procedures for affected students and parents
- [ ] Define roles and responsibilities for incident response
- [ ] Include FERPA compliance officer in the incident response team
- [ ] Establish a process for assessing the scope and impact of an education records breach
- [ ] Document all incidents involving education records, including near-misses
- [ ] Conduct post-incident reviews and implement lessons learned
- [ ] Test the incident response plan at least annually through tabletop exercises
- [ ] Know how to file a complaint with the Student Privacy Policy Office if needed

---

## De-Identification and Research

- [ ] Establish standards for de-identifying education records
- [ ] Ensure de-identification goes beyond removing names (consider re-identification risk)
- [ ] Implement small-cell suppression to prevent identification in small populations
- [ ] Document de-identification methods used for each data release
- [ ] For research disclosures, ensure written agreements addressing data security and destruction
- [ ] Limit research access to the minimum data necessary
- [ ] Prohibit researchers from attempting to re-identify students
- [ ] Review all data releases for re-identification risk before disclosure

---

## Records Retention and Destruction

- [ ] Develop a records retention schedule that complies with FERPA and state law
- [ ] Ensure disclosure logs are retained for as long as the education records they reference
- [ ] Implement secure destruction procedures for education records that have met retention requirements
- [ ] Document all record destruction activities
- [ ] Include electronic records and backups in the retention and destruction schedule
- [ ] Ensure vendors destroy or return education records upon contract termination
- [ ] Verify vendor destruction through certificates of destruction or equivalent documentation
